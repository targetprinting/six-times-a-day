The structure of Six Times a Day

This epic story is divided in various ways, hopefully making it easier to read. Omitting the support files (of which this is one), this story, which spans 76 days in late 2002, consists of 153 parts, almost all of which contain between 20,000 and 35,000 words of story text. It has been found that smaller parts are more managable for the reader, so most of the latter days in the story each extend over multiple parts.

Each part is, by itself, the size of a typical novella. If it were printed as a paperback pocketbook, each single part would fill 50 to 100 pages of text, and more when the many illustrations are included.

Each part has a title that is the name of a classic rock song, chosen to match the part's thematic content. Each song was popular before the time in late 2002 when the story takes place.

Each part is partitioned into between seven and fifteen chapters, each usually between 1000 and 4000 words long. Each chapter has one or more color illustrations.

A drop-down menu at the beginning of each part provides direct links to each chapter in the part, as well as listing the principal characters who appear in that chapter. Characters whose names are preceded by a lower-case "d" appear in daydreams but not otherwise (unless also listed without the prefixing "d").

Each part begins with an illustration of the primary women in Alan's life. New women appear in the illustration in the part in which they are introduced, to enable the reader to visualize them and recognize them once they start showing up in the story. In the near future, clicking on a woman's face will bring up a short description of her, which some readers may find useful to better visualize the character.

The goal has been for each part to average one illustration per 1000 words, but the current average is closer to one illustration per 1350 words. For more on how the illustrations were made, click this Artwork link, which can also be found at the top-left and bottom-left of this page.

Before 2012, most of the illustrations for parts 120 and earlier were grayscale. Those have now all been colorized, mostly through the effort of longtime-proofreader YamiBoy to whom we are all grateful.
