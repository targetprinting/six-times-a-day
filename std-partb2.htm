Changes since 2011 in Six Times a Day (6TD)

Bust sizes, rather than just bra-cup sizes, are now specified for the women. Many of these are changed from the prior version of 6TD for two reasons:

  • the bra cup sizing system has been changed from a complex U.K. system to a more-readily-understood U.S. system, which is consistent with 6TD occurring on the U.S. West Coast; and
     
  • the band and bra sizes of some of the larger-busted women have changed to reflect their height and proportional rib-cage and chest sizes, as well as their individual genetic heritage.

All changes in bust size were made on the advice of a similarly large-busted young woman who has recently joined the 6TD Staff, followed by much discussion on this forum thread.

Houses and grounds

Why this addition? The effort to revise 6TD accelerated in 2011, when many more missing scenes were added to the early parts. In early 2012 a new team of proofreaders was recruited to review the revised text, identify inconsistencies within the story or between the text and illustrations, and recommend corrections to either or both. Within a month or two this had progressed to the point where it became critical to reconcile the various descriptions of the Plummer house and grounds. One new proofreader, Magoo, took on the task of uncovering all the constraints on the Plummer house. Another new proofreader, Mick, used those constraints to create a plausible house plan that could meet structural and building code requirements. After many iterations this converged to the Plummer house plan found below.

Once the Plummer house plan had largely stabilized, Magoo reviewed all the illustrations for the revised parts and identified their context in the 6TD text, which Spacer then used to reconcile the image backgrounds and the text where possible.

This process is expected to continue as other parts are revised. It is planned to apply the same process to the grounds of the Plummer and Pestridge houses, and to those parts of the Pestridge house that play an important role in 6TD.

These designs are preliminary and incomplete. They will be updated whenever newer versions are available. The designations of the floors as "upper" and "ground" attempt to avoid the differences in floor nomenclature between the U.S. and many other parts of the world, which disagree on which floor would be correctly labeled the "first" floor.
