The structure of Six Times a Day

This epic story is divided in various ways, hopefully making it easier to read. Omitting the five support files (of which this is one), this story currently consists of 119 parts, each of which contains between 19,500 and 40,000 words of story text. It has been found that smaller parts are more managable for the reader, so many parts have been split in two. That is why some parts have "A" or "B" suffixes to their names.

Each part is, by itself, the size of a typical novella. If it were printed as a paperback pocketbook, each single part would fill 50 to 100 pages of text, and more when the many illustrations are included.

Each part has a title that is the name of a classic rock song, chosen to match the part's thematic content. Each song was popular before the time in 2002 when the story takes place.

Each part is partitioned into between seven and seventeen chapters, each usually between 1000 and 4000 words, and each with one or more illustrations.

The goal has been for each part to average one illustration per 1000 words, but the current average is closer to one illustration per 1275 words. For more on how the illustrations were made, click the Artwork link at the top or bottom of this page.

In prior versions of this story, most of the illustrations for parts 64 and earlier were grayscale, but they have now been converted to color, mostly through the effort of longtime-reader YamiBoy, to whom we are all grateful.
